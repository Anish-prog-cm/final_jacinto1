Asia

The continent of Asia is home to various traditions and cultures. From these practices stem stories of mythical beasts. Below are 3 of these stories. They span from China, Japan, and to Korea.

Pixiu

The Pixiu is a mythical hybrid creature from China with the status of a guardian. They resemble strong lions with wings and protect thos who practice feng shui. It has a very strong appetite for jewels. A female Pixiu, also called Bìxié, is identified for having two antlers. Meanwhile, Tiānlù is the name for a male Pixiu, which has one antler.

Tengu

The Tengu is a Japanese supernatural creature, at times thought to be the reincarnated spirit of an arrogant person. They are described to be mischievious and live in the trees of mountainous area. Tengu have a reputation for great swordsmanship. They are led by a chief who is many times depicted with a vexed expression, eminent nose, robes, and a feather fan.

https://unsplash.com/photos/Z42-oWFtOsU

Kumiho

A Kumiho is a fox creature that appears in many folktales of Korea and similar to other creatures like the Huli Jing of China. The Kumiho can transform into a woman and aims to lure men to eat their heart or liver. Kumihos are said to live for thousands of years. They have a menacing demeanor and a want to hunt humans for their meal.

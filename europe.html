Europe

The European continent is home to various traditions and cultures. From these practices stem stories of mythical beasts. Below are 3 of these stories. They span from celtic to english folkore and on.

Griffin

The Griffin is a mythical beast best known for being hald lion and half eagle. It is often seen as very ferocious and takes the role of a guardian. It typically watches over the forests, but some stroies say it watches over treasures and gold. Overtime, it has been adopted as a symbol for many purposes due to its ornamental nature.

A Puck is a solitary being. Ironically, Puck is a domesticized nature spirit. Many times they are said to do housework and help with chores. However, if made upset, Puck can be michievous.

Selkie

The Selkie calls the sea home. From Celtic and Norse mythology, a Selkie is a creature that can change from seal to human form. They do so by shedding their furs/skin. Often there are tales of humans stealing the selkie's skin so it can no longer return to sea and is coerced to stay on shore as human.

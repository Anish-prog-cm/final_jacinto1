Africa

Africa is home to various diverse traditions and cultures. From these practices stem stories of mythical beasts. Below are 3 of these stories. They span from Morocco, Egypt, and the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Aicha Kandicha

Aicha Kandicha is a creature is mythical creature from Moroccan folklore. Similar to a jinn, she is often depicted as beautiful and has legs in the form of a goat-like animal. She generally lives near a water source. Myths say she lures people with her beauty to kill them or drive them insane.

Apopis

Apopis is the demon of chaos and foe of the ancient Egyptian Sun god, Re. He is often depicted in the form of a serpent and symbolizing a threatening evil. Myhts say that Apopis could not be permanently subdued, even by Seth, guardian of Re. He has a continuous rivalry with Re.

https://unsplash.com/photos/EPLrqHHu2WI

Mokele-mbembe

Mokele-mbembe is an aquatic creature. It's name means "one who stops the flow of rivers" in Lingala. It is described by those who've claimed to see it as a four-legged herbivore. It also has a long neck and one tooth.

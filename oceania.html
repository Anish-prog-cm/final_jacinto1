Oceania

Oceania encompasses many different peoples and islands. It houses a multitude of stories and languages. From Oceania's cultures stem stories of mythical beings. Below are just 3 examples.

Rogo-Tumu-Here

Rogo-Tumu-Here is a fearsome creature of the deep. It is said to be a tremendous size. In fact, it was said to have attacked and eaten the wife of a deity. Rogo-Tumu-Here was slain by the deity Hina-Arau-Riki and Tu-Nui-Ka-Rere.

Nightmarchers

Nightmarchers emerge from Hawaiian mythology. They are the guards for sacred royalty and the ghost of ancient Hawaiian warriors. Stories tells of how they are summoned from their burial sites on the nights of Kanaloa or when honoring Hawaiian gods Kāne, Kū, Lono. They are human sized, wearing their weapons and gear, but their feet never touch the ground.

Yowie

Yowie approaches from the Australian outback. With roots in Aboriginal oral story-telling, Yowie is said to be hairy and ape-like. It has a large stature and enourmous feet. Some say that Yowie is aggressive, while other accounts describe it as timid.

North America

North America is home to various traditions and cultures. From these practices stem stories of mythical beasts. Many come from early indegnous people. Below are 3 of these stories.

Wendigo

The Wendigo is an evil mythological spirit. It's origin traces back to the indigenous people of the Great Lakes region and some of the First Nations. It is many times described with human and deer or animal-like characteristics. It provokes people to have evil thoughts and even murderous actions once under its influence.

Pukwudgie

Pukwudgies are gremline-like creatures. They have a very short stature, with some sources saying 2 to 3ft tall. They come from the stories of the Lenape people. They are said to lurk forests and transform into animals for michief.

Chepi

The Chepi originates from the Narragansett tribe of the east coast. It is a fairy or ghost. Full of knowledge, the Chepi shares infromation with medicine people in the form of visions or dreams. The medicine people may also summon them to fight against an opoosing supernatural force.

South America

The mythos of the Americas continues here. South America is home to a multitude of cultures. From these cultures stem stories of mythical beings. Below are just 3 examples.

Curupira

The Curupira is mythical being of Tupi-Guarani origin. It's name means "covered in blisters". The Curupira has man-like qualities but with orange hair and backwards feet. Curupira misleads travelers of the forest by leaving confusing footprints pointing one way while moving the other with its backwards feet.

Dzoavits

Dzoavits is an ogre-like demon. Coming from Shoshonean mythology, Dzoavits was a being who committed aggregious acts like stealing children. He even stole the sun. In various stories, it is said he confronts many mythological beasts beofre trapping himself in a cave.

The Cu Bird

The Cu Bird is an aviary being of vanity. The story goes that the bird was once very upset by its own appearance, so much so that the Owl took pity on him. The Owl convinced other birds to donate a feather each to the Cu in exchange for the Cu becoming a messenger. In the end, the Cu became infatuated with its looks and neglected his role. The Cu and the Owl then became designated as night birds, never to appear in the day.
